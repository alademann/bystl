Rules and Regulations

1. ELIGIBILITY
The championship is open to all individuals 11 years and older. Men, women and youths ages 11 to 17 will compete separately. All competitors will need to provide a birth certificate, drivers license, other legal document with photo identification showing proof of age. The champion and runner up for each division will represent Missouri at the National Yoga Asana Championship to be held in Los Angeles. The third place finisher will serve as an alternate in case the champion or runner up is unable to compete in the national championship.

2. PROHIBITED
No stimulating drug or alcoholic beverage can be used before or during the competition by any competitor. Any such use will be treated as violation of the rules. No watches, clocks or timers are allowed on or near the competitor. The World Yoga Foundation has full right to suspend a competitor.

3. ENTRY FEE
The entry fee for participating in the Missouri Regional Yoga Championship is $25, payable by cash, check or money order. Please make check payable to our sponsor, Bikram Yoga South Kansas City.

4. APPLICATION
Complete the Entry Form and submit it with the following:
1. $25 Entry fee
2. Copy of photo ID or other proof of age, and
3. Signed Waiver/Release of Liability form.

5. ATTIRE
Choose an outfit that best enhances the body’s shape and proportions.
Females: One or two-piece leotard or exercise outfit which exposes the arms and legs (no bikinis please).
Males: Speedo style swim suit or cycle shorts.
Note: Please minimize accessories and do not wear a headband, wrist bands, a bandanna or watch. Unobtrusive jewelry is permissible.

6. JUDGES
Judges are appointed from a list of qualified and certified judges prepared by the World Yoga Foundation. All selected judges will be brought in from outside the state of Missouri. Relatives of a judge cannot participate in a competition the judge is officiating. Coaches should not officiate at competition in which one of their subjects is competing.

7. FORMAT OF COMPETITION
When the competitor’s name is called he or she will walk to the middle of the stage, bring hands to namaskar greeting (prayer position in center of chest), bow to the judges and audience and then release hands to the sides. Instructions will be given by the words, “Start please,” for competitor to begin. The three (3) minute timing will start when the competitor begins his/her movement into the 1st posture. He/she will have three (3) minutes within which to completely execute the five (5) compulsory postures as detailed below and the two (2) additional postures of the competitor’s choice. Each posture must be announced by the competitor before beginning to execute the posture. Further instructions are included below. All competitors should consult with a coach, judge or someone who is familiar with these procedures prior to entering the competition so as to assure a smooth and well presented performance. Additional information and instruction can be retrieved from our sponsor, Bikram Yoga Memphis.

8. ASANAS
All competitors over the age of 18 will perform a total of seven (7) asanas, five (5) compulsory and two (2) additional asanas of the competitor’s choosing from the classic 84 asanas as derived from Patanjali, are all to be completed in the following order within three (3) minutes (Please contact our sponsor, Bikram Yoga Memphis, for information on the Youth Division postures).
1. Standing-Head-to-Knee Pose (Dandayamana Janushirasana), executed in four (4) distinct phases
2. Standing Bow Pulling Pose (Dandayamana Dhanurasana)
3. Bow Pose (Dhanurasana)
4. Rabbit Pose (Sasangasana)
5. Stretching Pose (Paschimottanasana)
6. Additional Choice Posture
7. Additional Choice Posture

9. JUDGING CRITERIA
80 points maximum can be awarded to each competitor. Those 80 points are broken down as follows:
A. Grace and Composure: 10 points maximum can be awarded for the overall grace and composure of the competitor. For example, judges may consider the following aspects in this category: the energy being projected by the competitor; personality; vitality; the competitor’s gait when walking on the stage and movement between and during the postures; the overall grace with which the competitor conducts himself or herself; level of confidence; charisma; timing; calmness of breath; etc.
B. Execution of Postures: 10 points maximum can be awarded for the execution of each of the seven (7) postures. Balance, strength, flexibility, timing and normal breathing are aspects inherent in the proper execution of asanas. Points (in whole or half point increments at the judges’ discretion) can be deducted for the following: wobbling or lack of stability in balance postures; inability to fully execute posture to its maximum expression; hesitation or reversal of motion once posture has been started; lack of proper alignment; poor timing and flow of posture; lack of complete stillness or demonstration of complete control once fully executed; etc.
