Class Tips - A Few Rules and Yoga Etiquette close
 

In order to create a pleasant environment and peaceful yoga experience for everyone please take a moment and read the following information:

Necessities Water, a yoga mat, and a large towel are required.
(All 3 are available for rent or purchase)
Wear lightweight, close fitting workout clothes and bare feet.
Shorts are preferred over long pants.
Time All classes are 90 minutes, plan to stay in the class the entire time.
The studio will be open 30 minutes prior to and 30 minutes after each class.
Please arrive at least 10 minutes prior to the start of class. You should arrive early enough that you have time to settle into the classroom at least 5 minutes before the class starts.
First-time students must arrive at least 15 minutes early to register. This includes visiting Bikram Yoga students, regardless of previous experience.
Latecomers will not be admitted into class. No exceptions
Before Class Sign in and print legibly. (first and last name)
Please do not sign anyone else into class. Practice patience during check-in. If you are early, you shouldn’t have to worry!
During Class What IS allowed in the yoga room:
Mat, towel, water. Inhalers, tissues, and a good attitude.

What is NOT allowed in the yoga room:
Talking. Shoes, Socks, Cellular Phones, Pagers, Watches, Keys, Wallets, Extra Bags, etc. No glass bottles or colored water/sports drinks. No Bean and Cheese Burritos. No gum. No excessive jewelry. No hangovers.

The teacher will indicate the points during class when everyone takes their water breaks.
Please refrain from wiping away your sweat throughout class.
Practice stillness between postures.
Honor the silence of the room by moving quietly and keeping water bottles quiet.
Focus on only yourself in the front mirror. Beginners: Feel free to watch the people in front of you if you are lost or confused. However, be aware and please do not STARE. Gaze at yourself in the front mirror when sitting out a posture.
Grab Kleenex before class. If you need more during the class, let the instructor know and they will bring it to you. Tuck tissues under your mat during class, and take them with you when you leave.
The front row is reserved for veteran students who tend to not sit down & who follow the rules of this studio. Remember, you are leading the energy in the room! Beginners: Sit towards the back of the room until you feel comfortable and strong enough to move forward.
After Class Please use the recycling bin in the front lobby for all plastic bottles and empty ZICO containers.
Showers are a convenience to quickly rinse off. We have 1 in each locker room. Please be considerate of those waiting behind you.
Leaving
During Class		 Do your best to stay in the yoga room once class begins.
Please let the instructor know if an emergency arises during class (i.e. vomiting, bleeding, head is falling off). They will advise you of an appropriate time to leave if necessary. Leaving and entering the classroom should be done in between poses.
Breathing During class, don't forget to breathe! Breathe in and out through your nose, don't huff and puff through your mouth - this actually can cause dizziness!
Closing
Savasana		 Please stay to the end of class and enjoy a nice long Savasana. When class is over and AFTER the teacher leaves, please leave the room quietly, this is respectful of your neighbors who prefer a longer final Savasana.
Enjoy the peace and energy you're taking with you from class, and make up your mind to come back tomorrow!
Frequency In order to be effective, yoga should be practiced on a regular basis with no long lapses of time between lessons. A minimum commitment of 10 classes in 30 days is recommended to derive maximum benefit from this yoga program.
Talking Please visit with your friends in the lobby or changing areas, not in the yoga room. Please keep visiting in the hallway at a low volume.
Cell Phones Turn off cell phones and pagers and leave them in the changing area.
No cell phones or pagers allowed in the yoga room at any time.
Personal
Belongings		 Please leave all bags in the changing room. The studio door is locked during class or there is someone manning the lobby to ensure the safety of your things.
Please note: Bikram Yoga St. Louis is not responsible for lost or stolen items.
Cold or Flu? If you are sick, for example with the flu, cough, or fever, please stay home and rest.
Drainage or a head cold may be helped by taking class.
Taking anti-biotics or other medications may affect your class. Please speak to an instructor if you have any questions or concerns.
Yoga Mat Clean your yoga mat after ever use. Spray tea tree oil mixed with water on your mat, scrub, and air dry. Biweekly: Wash your mat in the tub, or washing machine on gentle.
Hygiene/
Perfume		 Please keep your body and feet clean when attending class.
Please refrain from wearing perfume, cologne, or other strong scents. This includes the stink of cigarette smoke!! Rinse your body prior to class if necessary.
Deodorant is okay!
Hydration/
Nutrition		 If you are not properly hydrated and/or did not get a chance to eat enough, please do not take class. Proper hydration and nourishment are required. Drink plenty of water throughout the day before class. Proper hydration helps your body flush away toxins and keeps your brain sharp!

Please have an empty stomach prior to the start of class. Make sure you are receiving adequate electrolyte intake: Calcium, Magnesium, Sodium, and Potassium. Sample drinks that contain electrolytes include: ZICO, Gatorade, Emergen-C, Vitamin Water, Smartwater.* *Note: Water only in the yoga room, please.
Chewing Gum For your safety gum chewing is not allowed during class.
